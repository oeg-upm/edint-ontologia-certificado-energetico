Energy Certificate: Description back to ToC

Figure 1 shows the conceptual model of the ontology with its classes and relationships (object properties).

The ontology models energy performance certificates for buildings according to Royal Decree 390/2021. Each certificate has an energy rating (with primary energy, CO₂ emissions, demands and consumption by end-use), an energy label (letter A–G) and a calculation procedure (CEP, CEH, CE3X, etc.).

The certificate is issued by a competent technician and may be delivered to the owner or tenant via user recommendations. If the building has room for improvement, the certificate includes improvement recommendations (on envelope, installations, automation or combined), with estimated savings, cost, payback period and the resulting improved label.

Classifications (certificate type, building use, climatic zone, energy source, procedure type, rating letter, rating scale version, recommendation type, recipient, data basis, access path and course module) are represented via object properties pointing to SKOS concepts, organized in independent concept schemes available in the kos/ folder.

The certificate may have a predecessor certificate (renewal or phase change: project → completed work) and, in the case of completed work, it is indicated whether the same version of the calculation procedure was used (Art. 9.4 RD 390/2021).
The example above shows a portion of the RDF knowledge graph for an existing building energy certificate. The certificate (type ExistingBuilding per SKOS) is issued by a competent technician and contains an energy rating (letter D, scale version RD 390/2021, calculated basis) and an energy label (letter D). Building data includes residential use, climatic zone D3 and useful surface. Classifications (certificate type, building use, climatic zone, rating letter, procedure type, data basis, recommendation type, recipient) are represented via SKOS concepts from the schemes available in kos/. The improvement recommendation (installation type) estimates an improvement to letter B with 38% savings.
